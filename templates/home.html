Our Mission

Our mission is to revolutionize fabric quality and dimensional stability by addressing the issue of fabric contraction, a common source of customer dissatisfaction. In textile manufacturing, woven fabrics undergo various stresses, resulting in contraction that affects their stability and appearance. The weaving process, especially the reed specifications, plays a crucial role in fabric contraction and depends on key fabric parameters. Traditional approaches to determining reed specifications have relied on empirical methods, often yielding less accurate results. As the textile industry embraces digital transformation, our study leverages machine learning to improve these processes.

Research Focus: Analyzing Fabric Specifications and Reed Parameters

In this study, we analyze the influence of fabric specifications—such as warp and weft yarn counts and ends and picks per inch—on reed specifications (namely, reed space and reed count) and on contraction in both the warp and weft directions. Understanding these relationships helps in predicting contraction more accurately, allowing for better quality control in fabric manufacturing.

Methodology Machine Learning for Predicting Reed Specifications

To achieve accurate predictions, we evaluated five machine learning algorithms: XGBoost, CatBoost, LGBM, Gradient Boosting Regressor (GBR), and Random Forest. By analyzing RMSE values and feature importance, we identified LGBM as the most effective model for predicting reed specifications. Additionally, we applied data augmentation through SMOTE to enhance model accuracy further, refining predictions for both fabric contraction and reed specifications.

Advancing Textile Manufacturing Through Predictive Analytics

Leveraging predictive analytics in textile manufacturing marks a substantial step toward solving longstanding quality control challenges, such as fabric contraction. By replacing traditional, empirical methods with data-driven approaches, textile companies can achieve higher accuracy in reed specifications, reducing errors that affect product dimensions and quality. Machine learning models, like the LGBM identified in this study, provide robust predictions that allow manufacturers to optimize production processes with a level of precision that was previously unattainable.


The findings of this research not only contribute to improved product consistency but also empower industry stakeholders with actionable insights that can enhance operational efficiency and customer satisfaction. Implementing predictive analytics helps streamline processes, minimize waste, and promote sustainable practices within textile manufacturing. Additionally, the successful application of techniques like data augmentation and feature analysis demonstrates the potential of machine learning to continually refine and improve predictive outcomes.
